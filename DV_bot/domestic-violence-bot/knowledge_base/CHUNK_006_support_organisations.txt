WHERE TO GET HELP IN UGANDA — SUPPORT ORGANISATIONS

If you or someone you know is experiencing domestic violence, these organisations can help. All services listed are FREE.

---

FIDA UGANDA (Uganda Association of Women Lawyers)
Type: Legal Aid NGO
What they do: Free legal advice, court representation, counselling, mediation, help with protection orders
Toll-free: 0800 111 511 (free on MTN and Airtel — trained lawyers answer)
Phone: +256 414 530 848
Email: fida@fidauganda.org
Website: www.fidauganda.or.ug
Address: Plot 100, Lutaya Drive, Nakawa Division, Kampala
Hours: Monday–Friday, 8:30 AM – 5:00 PM
Coverage: 27+ offices nationwide
Safety note: Conversations are confidential. Best first call for legal help.

---

UGANDA POLICE FORCE — CHILD AND FAMILY PROTECTION UNIT (CFPU) / GENDER DESK
Type: Government — Law Enforcement
What they do: Receive domestic violence reports, arrest abusers, ensure medical care, help with protection orders
GBV Toll-free: 0800 199 195 (free — GBV-specific helpline)
Emergency: 999
Additional lines: +256 707 772 691 / +256 776 478 803
CFPU direct: 0800 199 033
Available: 24 hours, 7 days a week
Coverage: CFPU desks at EVERY police station in Uganda
Safety note: Ask specifically for the "Gender Desk" or "Child and Family Protection Unit." You have the right to have your statement taken by an officer of the same sex as you.

---

JUSTICE CENTRES UGANDA (JCU)
Type: Government Legal Aid (within the Judiciary)
What they do: Free legal advice, court representation, mediation, self-representation support
Toll-free: 0800 100 210 (free)
Additional: 0753 205 029 / 0774 910 812
Address: Chief Magistrate Court, Mengo, 2nd Floor, Kampala
Website: www.justicecentres.go.ug
Hours: Monday–Friday, 8:30 AM – 5:00 PM
Coverage: Multiple centres across Uganda (Mengo, Tororo, Lira, Hoima, Jinja, Masaka)
Safety note: Good alternative if FIDA is unavailable. Can provide actual court representation.

---

UGANDA WOMEN'S NETWORK (UWONET)
Type: Advocacy NGO
What they do: Referrals, GBV survivor support, advocacy, community programmes
Address: Plot 710, Block 216, Mirembe Close, Bbuye Kigowa – Ntinda, Kampala
Website: www.uwonet.or.ug
Hours: Monday–Friday, office hours
Safety note: UWONET is an advocacy organisation. For direct legal help, contact FIDA or JCU first. UWONET is excellent for referrals and connecting with community support groups.

---

CENTRE FOR DOMESTIC VIOLENCE PREVENTION (CEDOVIP)
Type: Community Prevention NGO
What they do: Psychosocial counselling, community education, GBV survivor support, SASA! programme
Address: Plot 16, Tufnell Drive, Kamwokya, Kampala
Hours: Monday–Friday, office hours
Safety note: Best for counselling and emotional support at the community level. Works closely with Raising Voices Uganda.

---

UGANDA HUMAN RIGHTS COMMISSION (UHRC)
Type: Independent Government Body
What they do: Investigate human rights violations, mediate complaints, order remedies
Toll-free: 0800 200 500 (free)
Website: www.uhrc.ug
Address: Buganda Road, Kampala
Hours: Monday–Friday, 8:00 AM – 5:00 PM
Safety note: Use UHRC if police or courts have failed to act on your complaint, or if a government official is involved in the abuse.
