TYPES OF DOMESTIC VIOLENCE UNDER UGANDAN LAW

PHYSICAL ABUSE
Any act that causes bodily pain, harm, or danger to life, limb, or health — or that damages the victim's health or development. Includes assault, criminal intimidation, and criminal force.
Examples: hitting, kicking, slapping, punching, biting, pushing, strangling, throwing objects.

SEXUAL ABUSE
Any unwanted sexual act or conduct that humiliates the victim, including sexual assault or rape. This includes MARITAL RAPE — it is illegal for a partner to force sex on their spouse.
Examples: forced sexual intercourse, pressure to perform sexual acts, any sexual contact without free consent.

EMOTIONAL, VERBAL, AND PSYCHOLOGICAL ABUSE
A REPEATED pattern of degrading or humiliating conduct. Must be repeated — not a single incident.
Includes: repeated insults or name-calling, repeated threats to cause emotional pain, extreme jealousy or possessiveness that invades the victim's privacy or freedom.
Also: committing any act of domestic violence in front of a child — this counts as abuse OF that child too.
Examples: constantly telling a partner they are worthless, controlling who they can speak to, screaming threats in front of children.

ECONOMIC ABUSE
Depriving the victim of financial resources or property they are entitled to, or blocking their access to household resources.
Includes: withholding money for food, rent, or children's needs; selling or disposing of the victim's property without consent; stopping the victim from working or earning money; refusing to pay court-ordered maintenance.

HARASSMENT
A repeated pattern of conduct intended to cause fear, annoyance, or distress.
Includes: repeatedly watching or hanging around where the victim lives or works; making repeated abusive calls; repeatedly sending threatening messages (texts, emails, letters); repeatedly following or chasing the victim.

INTIMIDATION
Uttering a threat, or causing the victim to receive a threat, that makes them afraid.
Examples: threatening to harm the victim if they report, threatening to destroy property, sending threats through other people.

ALL of the above are recognised as domestic violence under the Uganda Domestic Violence Act, 2010.
