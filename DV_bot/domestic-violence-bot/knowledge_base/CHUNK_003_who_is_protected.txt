WHO DOES THE LAW PROTECT?

You do NOT have to be married for the law to protect you. The Uganda Domestic Violence Act, 2010 protects anyone in a "domestic relationship." This includes:

SPOUSES
Husband and wife — whether in a civil, customary, or religious marriage.

FORMER SPOUSES
People who are separated or divorced. The law still protects you even after the relationship has ended.

COHABITING PARTNERS
People who live together in the same home, whether or not they are married.

FAMILY MEMBERS
People related by blood (e.g. siblings, adult children, parents, grandparents) or by marriage (e.g. in-laws).

SHARED HOUSEHOLD MEMBERS
Anyone who shares the same home — including extended family living together.

CHILDREN
Children living in the home are protected. Importantly, a child who WITNESSES domestic violence — even if the violence is not directed at them — is legally considered a victim under the Act.

KEY POINT: The law covers people of ALL genders. Men can also be victims of domestic violence and are protected under the Act.

If you are experiencing abuse from someone in any of the above relationships, the Uganda Domestic Violence Act applies to your situation.
