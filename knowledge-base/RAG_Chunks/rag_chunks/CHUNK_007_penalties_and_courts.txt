PENALTIES FOR DOMESTIC VIOLENCE IN UGANDA

Under Section 4 of the Uganda Domestic Violence Act, 2010, a person convicted of domestic violence faces:

FINE: Up to 48 currency points — equivalent to approximately UGX 960,000 (around USD 260)
IMPRISONMENT: Up to 2 years in prison
BOTH: The court can impose both a fine and imprisonment
COMPENSATION: The court can additionally order the abuser to pay compensation to the victim. The amount is set by the judge based on: pain and suffering, nature and severity of the violence, medical expenses incurred, and other harm caused to the victim.

BREACHING A PROTECTION ORDER: If an abuser breaks the conditions of a protection order, this is a separate criminal offence carrying the same penalties — up to UGX 960,000 fine or 2 years in prison.

WHERE DOMESTIC VIOLENCE CASES ARE HEARD

LOCAL COUNCIL COURTS (LC1, LC2, LC3)
The first point of contact for many victims. Can hear complaints, make various orders, and refer serious cases to police or magistrate's court. Found in every community.

MAGISTRATE'S COURT
Handles the majority of domestic violence cases — both civil (protection orders) and criminal charges. Can issue interim and full protection orders. Three levels: Chief Magistrate, Grade I, and Grade II.

FAMILY AND CHILDREN COURT
Specifically handles family matters including domestic violence — especially where children are involved. Has the same powers as a magistrate's court for protection orders.

HIGH COURT
Hears appeals from magistrate's courts. Has unlimited original jurisdiction — can handle any domestic violence case of any scale.

IMPORTANT FOR VICTIMS: You do not need a lawyer to make a complaint. You can report to your LC court or the police without any legal representation. Free legal help is available from FIDA Uganda (0800 111 511) or Justice Centres Uganda (0800 100 210).
