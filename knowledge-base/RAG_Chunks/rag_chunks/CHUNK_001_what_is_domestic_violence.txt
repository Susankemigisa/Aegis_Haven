WHAT IS DOMESTIC VIOLENCE?

Under Uganda's Domestic Violence Act, 2010, domestic violence is any act OR failure to act by a perpetrator that does any of the following:

1. Harms, injures, or endangers the health, safety, life, or wellbeing — whether mental or physical — of the victim. This includes physical abuse, sexual abuse, emotional abuse, verbal abuse, psychological abuse, and economic abuse.

2. Forces or threatens the victim (or someone related to them) to meet any unlawful demand for property or money.

3. Threatens the victim or anyone related to them.

4. Otherwise causes harm — physical or mental — to the victim.

IMPORTANT: The victim's consent is NOT a legal defence. Even if a victim did not resist or object, the abuser can still be charged and convicted under the Act (Section 5).

Domestic violence is a CRIME in Uganda. Any person in a domestic relationship who commits domestic violence can face a fine of up to UGX 960,000, up to 2 years in prison, or both — plus court-ordered compensation to the victim.
